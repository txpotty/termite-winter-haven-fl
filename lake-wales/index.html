Our Comprehensive Termite Inspection Process

The first and most crucial step in effective termite control is a thorough inspection. Our experienced technicians are trained to identify even the subtlest signs of termite activity. Visual Inspection of Key Areas

Our technicians will perform a detailed visual inspection of your property, both interior and exterior. We carefully examine common termite entry points and areas prone to moisture and wood contact. This includes foundation walls, crawl spaces, basements, attics, garages, and any wooden structures. We look for tell-tale signs like mud tubes, damaged wood, and discarded wings.

Using Advanced Detection Tools

In addition to visual inspection, we may use advanced detection tools to locate termites that are hidden within walls or other structures. These tools can include moisture meters, infrared cameras, and acoustic detectors. These technologies help us pinpoint the exact location and scope of the infestation, ensuring no termites are left undetected.

Customized Termite Treatment Plans

Once the inspection is complete, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to your specific needs. Every property and every infestation is unique, and a one-size-fits-all approach is not effective. Our treatment plans consider the type of termite, the severity of the infestation, the construction of your property, and your preferences.

Targeted Liquid Termite Treatments

One of the most common and effective methods for termite control is the application of liquid termiticide around the perimeter of your property. This creates a protective barrier that termites cannot penetrate. The termiticide is applied directly to the soil adjacent to the foundation, ensuring comprehensive coverage. Our technicians are expertly trained in the precise application of these treatments for maximum effectiveness.

Subterranean Termite Baiting Systems

For certain situations, especially with subterranean termites, baiting systems can be a highly effective long-term solution. Bait stations are strategically placed in the ground around your property. Foraging termites discover the bait and carry it back to their colony, eliminating the entire colony over time. This method can be less disruptive than liquid treatments and offers continuous monitoring.

Wood Treatments for Drywood Termites

Drywood termites can infest wooden structures directly, without needing contact with the soil. For these infestations, localized wood treatments are often necessary. This can involve injecting insecticides directly into infested wood or using fumigation in severe cases. Our technicians will assess the extent of the drywood termite activity and recommend the most appropriate treatment.

Conscious Pest Management: Common Termite Problems and Solutions

Understanding the common signs and problems associated with termites is the first step in protecting your property. Recognizing these indicators early can save you significant time and money on repairs. Here are some common issues people face with termites in Lake Wales, FL, and how our services provide effective solutions.

Mud Tubes on Foundation Walls

Mud tubes are one of the most visible signs of subterranean termites. These tunnel-like structures, made of soil and wood, are created by termites to travel between their underground colony and the wood source above ground, protecting them from open air and predators. Finding mud tubes on your foundation walls, crawl space walls, or even interior walls indicates active subterranean termite activity.

If you see mud tubes, do not disturb them immediately. Call us for a professional inspection. Our technicians can confirm if the tubes are active and assess the extent of the infestation. We will then implement targeted treatments to disrupt the termite's travel routes and eliminate the colony accessing your property through these tubes. Our liquid treatments around the foundation are particularly effective in creating a barrier that prevents termites from building and using mud tubes to reach your home.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites feed on the cellulose in wood, which can lead to significant damage over time. Often, the damage is not visible on the surface of the wood, as termites eat from the inside out. However, if you tap on wooden structures and they sound hollow, or if you notice blistering or darkening on wooden surfaces, it could be a sign of internal termite damage.

Identifying the extent of wood damage requires a professional assessment. Our technicians can perform a thorough inspection, including probing and sounding of wooden elements, to determine the areas affected by termites. Based on the type of termite and the severity of the damage, we will recommend the most appropriate treatment. This could involve localized wood treatments for drywood termites or comprehensive treatments for subterranean termites that are causing the internal damage. Early detection and treatment of damaged wood can prevent the spread of the infestation and reduce the need for extensive structural repairs down the line.

Discarded Termite Wings

Swarming is a natural part of the termite lifecycle, where reproductive termites (swarmers) leave the colony to establish new ones. This typically occurs during certain times of the year, often after rain. Swarmers are often mistaken for flying ants, but termites have straight antennae and waistlines, while ants have elbowed antennae and pinched waistlines. After a swarm, termites shed their wings, and finding piles of small, translucent wings near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures is a strong indicator of a nearby termite colony.

Finding discarded wings is a clear sign that termites have swarmed from a colony in or very close to your property. While the swarming itself may not cause damage, it confirms the presence of an active infestation that needs to be addressed. Our inspection will help locate the source of the swarm and the established colony. We will then implement targeted treatments to eliminate the colony and prevent future swarms from originating from your property. Ignoring discarded wings is a mistake; it's nature's alarm that you have a termite problem that is reproducing and expanding.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites create small, pellet-like droppings called frass. These droppings are often pushed out of small kick-out holes in the infested wood. Finding small piles of what looks like sawdust or sand near wooden furniture, window frames, or door frames can be a sign of drywood termite activity.

If you discover frass, it indicates that drywood termites are actively feeding within the wood. Our technicians can identify the type of termite by the appearance of the frass. Treatment for drywood termites typically involves directly treating the infested wood, which can involve injecting insecticides into the borer holes or, in more severe cases, fumigation of the structure. Addressing drywood termites early is essential to prevent extensive damage to valuable wooden items and structural components.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Significant termite damage can weaken the structural integrity of your property, leading to noticeable issues like sagging floors or ceilings. While these issues can be caused by other factors, termite damage is a common culprit, especially with long-standing infestations.

If you notice sagging floors or ceilings, it's critical to have a professional inspection immediately. This could indicate severe structural damage caused by termites. Our inspection will assess the extent of the damage and confirm if termites are the cause. If termites are responsible, we will develop a comprehensive treatment plan to eliminate the infestation and prevent further damage. Depending on the severity, structural repairs may be necessary after the termites are eradicated. Our priority is to stop the termite activity and then guide you on the necessary steps for repairing the damage to restore the structural integrity of your property.

Clicking Sounds in Walls

In some cases, you might hear faint clicking or tapping sounds coming from within your walls. This can be the sound of termites chewing on the wood. While not always audible, in quiet environments, it can be a sign of active termite feeding within your walls.

Hearing clicking sounds in your walls is a strong indicator of an active and potentially widespread termite infestation. Our advanced detection tools, such as acoustic detectors, can help confirm the presence and location of termite activity within the walls without causing damage. Once the locations are pinpointed, we can apply targeted treatments to the affected areas to eliminate the termites. This might involve injecting termiticides into wall voids or other methods depending on the specific situation and type of termite.
